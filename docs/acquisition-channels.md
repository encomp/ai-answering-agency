# Customer Acquisition Channels — Rated & Ranked

## Context
We are selling two products to garage door repair contractors:
1. Instant Quote Generator
2. AI Voice Answering Service

The audience: 1–5 truck owner-operators, often on job sites, using voicemail or personal cell.

---

## 1. Channel Assessment

### Tier 1 — High Intent, Local Credibility

#### A. Local Business Networking / In-Person Events
- **Examples**: chamber of commerce, local trade associations, franchisee meetings, equipment supplier open houses.
- **Cost**: $0–$50/event.
- **Why it works**: These owners buy from people they meet in person. Local presence removes the "scam/outsider" objection.
- **Effort**: Medium; requires showing up consistently.
- **Verdict**: Best initial validation channel. You can demo the voice agent live on a phone.

#### B. Facebook / Instagram Groups for Contractors
- **Examples**: garage door tech groups, local contractor groups, "Garage Door Talk" style forums.
- **Cost**: $0.
- **Why it works**: Owners ask "anyone use X for answering service?" daily.
- **Effort**: Medium; requires authentic participation, not spam.
- **Verdict**: Strong early channel, especially for social proof and case studies.

#### C. Google Ads — High-Intent Keywords
- **Keywords**: "garage door answering service," "after hours calls for garage door," "garage door lead capture," "garage door quoting tool."
- **Cost**: $5–$15 CPC; $500–$2,000/month budget.
- **Why it works**: Only reaches owners actively searching for help.
- **Effort**: Low friction once campaigns are live; requires landing page + offer.
- **Verdict**: Best paid channel once offer is proven. Use only after you have 2–3 case studies.

### Tier 2 — Scalable, Setup-Heavy

#### D. Partner Referrals — Complementary Vendors
- **Examples**: garage door software companies, POS/ticketing tools, CRMs for contractors, SEO agencies serving trades, insurance providers for contractors.
- **Cost**: $0 upfront; rev-share or flat referral fee ($50–$200/client).
- **Why it works**: They already trust the owner; you borrow credibility.
- **Effort**: High initial setup; requires outreach and partner agreements.
- **Verdict**: Best long-term channel; slower first deal.

#### E. Cold Email — Compliance-Safe
- **Method**: Scrape websites (not just Maps), find owner emails via Hunter/RocketReach, and send a 3-email sequence offering a free 14-day pilot.
- **Cost**: $0–$50/month for tools.
- **Why it works**: Less regulated than SMS; can reference their actual website.
- **Effort**: Medium; needs good copy and follow-up.
- **Verdict**: Good scaling channel if SMS is off the table. Expect 1–3% reply rates.

#### F. LinkedIn Outreach
- **Method**: Target "owner," "founder," "president" at garage door companies in your metro.
- **Cost**: $0–$100/month for Sales Navigator.
- **Why it works**: Many contractors post/review services on LinkedIn; also good for partner discovery.
- **Effort**: Medium; better for enterprise/HVAC than micro-contractors.
- **Verdict**: Secondary; not as strong as Facebook/groups or email.

### Tier 3 — Risky, High Effort, or Lower ROI

#### G. Cold SMS / Text blasts
- **Method**: Scrape Google Maps, filter cell numbers, send bulk texts via StraightText/Twilio.
- **Cost**: $50–$150 scrape + $20–$50 SMS.
- **Why it is attractive**: High open rates; fast feedback.
- **Why it is risky**: Post-2024 FCC TCPA ruling, texts to numbers without 1:1 prior consent are illegal. Scraped Google Maps numbers are not consent. This exposes you to $500–$1,500/violation fines, carrier filtering, and brand damage.
- **Verdict**: Not recommended unless you first obtain clear opt-in (e.g., "Text YES to get a free quote on AI answering"). Even then, treat as experimental.

#### H. Cold Calling
- **Why it is risky/rejected**: Time-consuming, low answer rates, many contractors screen unknown numbers.
- **Verdict**: Avoid unless you have a VA team and a script that converts cold calls into scheduled demos at >5%.

#### I. Google Maps Reviews / Listing Spam
- **Why avoid**: Unethical and violates Google TOS; risks your own Maps standing.
- **Verdict**: Hard no.

## 2. Recommended Mix for Garage Door Play

| Phase | Channel | Goal | Expected CAC |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Weeks 1–4** | Local networking + Facebook groups | 3–5 pilot clients | $50–$100 |
| **Weeks 5–8** | Cold email + demo content | 5–10 additional clients | $100–$200 |
| **Months 3–6** | Google Ads + partner referrals | 10–20 clients | $150–$300 |
| **Month 6+** | Case-study-driven inbound + affiliates | 20+ clients | <$100 |

## 3. Specific Tactics Worth Testing

### Demo-in-a-DM
- Record a 60-second Loom of the voice agent answering a fake garage door call.
- Send via FB Messenger or email: "I built this for a DFW garage door owner last week. Would it be useful for you?"
- Low friction; high perceived value.

### "Free First Lead" Offer
- "Free 14-day trial. I don't charge you unless you get at least one closed lead from the AI agent."
- Removes pricing objection entirely.

### Local Case Study
- Give the product for free to 1–2 local owners in exchange for:
  - a 60-second testimonial video,
  - permission to use their name/logo,
  - a before/after screenshot of missed vs. captured calls.
- This becomes your #1 conversion asset.

### Niche Event / Supplier Partnerships
- Offer the quoting tool free to garage door parts distributors in exchange for a mention to their customer list.
- Or, sponsor a local chapter meeting.

## 4. Key Warning on Cold Outreach

The video’s approach of scraping Maps and cold texting—while exciting to watch—carries material legal and deliverability risk today.

Preferred compliance-safe cold outreach:
- **Email**: scraped websites + verified business emails with clear opt-out.
- **Direct mail**: postcard to the business address with a QR code to a free trial.
- **Warm referral**: ask existing clients for one intro per client.
- **Inbound content**: short YouTube/TikTok/Reels showing "how I doubled a garage door company's after-hours leads" → funnel to book a call.
