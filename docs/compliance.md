# Compliance Notes for Outreach

## Status
This is a starter note, not legal advice. Consult a lawyer before launching any outreach campaign.

---

## 1. Key Regulations

| Regulation | Applies To | Core Rule |
|---|---|---|
| **TCPA** (47 U.S.C. § 227) | Calls + texts to US numbers | Prior express consent required for autodialed/text messages. Fines: $500–$1,500/violation. |
| **FCC 2024 Ruling** | Texts to consumers | Extended Do-Not-Call protections to texts; effectively banned many mass-text lead-gen models without 1:1 consent. |
| **CTIA guidelines** | SMS best practices | Requires opt-in, opt-out keywords, sender identification, and clear content. |
| **CAN-SPAM** | Commercial email | Requires clear opt-out, truthful headers/subjects, and physical mailing address. |
| **State laws** | Vary by state | Some states (e.g., California, Texas, Florida) have stricter telemarketing/robocall rules. |

---

## 2. Practical Implications for This Business

### Cold Email
- Safer than SMS/calls.
- Still requires CAN-SPAM-compliant headers, opt-out, and sender disclosure.
- Using scraped business emails may violate scraper/platform TOS even if not clearly illegal; prefer lists with clear usage rights.
- Avoid deceptive subject lines or impersonation ("I found your listing" is fine; "Call from IRS" is not).

### Cold SMS / Text
- Current posture: **not recommended without explicit opt-in**.
- Scraped numbers are **not consent**.
- Required if you do text: (1) clear opt-in, (2) opt-out mechanism, (3) sender registration (10DLC or short code), (4) opt-in records. Cost and setup are nontrivial.
- Consumer numbers > business numbers in sensitivity, but B2B texts are not fully exempt.

### Cold Calling
- Legal in most states for B2B if numbers are publicly available, but:
  - National DNC list still applies to certain calls.
  - State DNC and robocall laws may restrict auto-dialers.
  - Manual dialing by a live person is lowest risk.
- AI-generated voice calls add complexity; many states restrict "artificial" voices without disclosure.

### Voice AI Answering Service (Inbound)
- Lower risk because the **caller initiates**.
- Still recommended: announce AI presence if required by state ("This is an automated service...").
- Recording disclosure may be required in some states (beep or verbal notice).

---

## 3. Minimum Compliance Checklist

- [ ] Add opt-out instruction to every outbound email footer.
- [ ] Include physical business address in every outbound email.
- [ ] Do not send texts to scraped numbers unless you have express written consent.
- [ ] Review scraper/platform TOS for data use restrictions.
- [ ] Consult a US business lawyer before launching outreach.
- [ ] If using AI voice inbound: check state-by-state disclosure rules.
- [ ] Keep records: opt-in timestamp, message copy, sending phone numbers/domains.
